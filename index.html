Research

The Impact of Health Technology Assessment on Pharmaceutical Prices: Evidence from Germany
Giovanni Righetti
Accepted AIES Young Researchers Prize

This paper evaluates the effect of Germany's 2011 AMNOG reform — which introduced mandatory health technology assessment for newly launched drugs — on pharmaceutical prices. Using a difference-in-differences approach, I find significant effects on the pricing of new pharmaceuticals in Germany, with implications for other countries that reference German prices.

SSRN European Journal of Health Economics, forthcoming
Pricing Without Borders: Spillovers of External Reference Pricing in Pharmaceutical Regulation
with Simona Gamba and Paolo Pertile
Under Review

Many countries use external reference pricing (ERP), linking domestic drug prices to foreign benchmarks. We show theoretically that strategic firm responses to ERP can create cross-country spillovers with unintended consequences. Exploiting the introduction of ERP in Germany in 2011 in a difference-in-differences design, we find that this policy caused a 6.6% price increase in reference countries relative to non-reference markets. Results are robust across specifications, including an approach that accounts for confidential discounts.

Pricing Mechanisms for Multi-Indication Drugs
with Pedro Pita Barros and Leonor Sá
Working Paper

We study optimal pricing mechanisms when a single drug treats multiple medical conditions with different clinical benefits. We compare uniform pricing with indication-based pricing, analysing how each mechanism affects access, firm incentives, and overall welfare.

On the Long-Term Effect of Most Favored Nation Drug Pricing
with Pedro Pita Barros and Leonor Sá
Submitted

We analyse the long-term effects of most favored nation pricing policies in pharmaceutical markets, exploring how such clauses interact with dynamic pricing incentives and cross-country competition.
